About

Xinyuan is a graduate student specializing in Consumer Behavior at the Stephen M. Ross School of Business, University of Michigan. Her research interests include consumer journeys, consumer identity, artificial intelligence, and anthropomorphism. Currently, her work focuses on exploring how "travel companions" enhance the customer journey beyond merely influencing outcomes. She also examines the dynamics of customer journeys in joint decision-making contexts, employing a multidisciplinary approach that integrates diverse theories and methods to analyze social influences.

Prior to joining the Ross School of Business, Xinyuan served as a Research Assistant at Luohan Academy, an open research institute founded by Alibaba Group and guided by distinguished social scientists, including seven Nobel Laureates in Economics. In this role, she conducted advanced data analysis, consumer segmentation, and fintech impact assessments, leveraging a combination of quantitative methodologies and qualitative insights to support strategic decision-making.
